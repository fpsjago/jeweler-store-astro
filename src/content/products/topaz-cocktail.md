### The Imperial Color

True imperial topaz exhibits a captivating golden-orange hue that's immediately recognizable:

**Color Description:**
- Base color: Rich golden orange
- Secondary tones: Peachy-pink undertones
- Saturation: Strong and vivid
- Pleochroism: Shows different colors from different angles

**What Makes It Special:**
The imperial designation is reserved for topaz with this specific color. Unlike common blue or white topaz (which is often treated), imperial topaz's color is entirely natural and much more rare.

**Color Shift:**
One of imperial topaz's most fascinating properties is pleochroism – it displays different colors when viewed from different angles. Turn your hand and watch the stone shift from golden-orange to peachy-pink to reddish tones.

### Brazilian Origin

The Ouro Preto region of Brazil produces the world's finest imperial topaz:

**Historical Significance:**
- Name "Ouro Preto" means "black gold" in Portuguese
- Region has produced gemstones for over 300 years
- Imperial topaz discovered there in the 18th century
- Associated with Brazilian royalty

**Current Status:**
- Limited production from selective mining
- Large, clean stones increasingly rare
- Prices rising due to scarcity
- Most valuable topaz variety

### Care Instructions

**Daily Care:**
- Remove during heavy physical activity
- Avoid impacts (topaz has cleavage planes)
- Remove before gardening or sports
- Clean regularly to maintain brilliance

**Cleaning:**
- Warm soapy water and soft brush
- Ultrasonic cleaning safe
- Avoid steam cleaning
- Professional cleaning recommended annually

**Storage:**
- Store in individual padded slot
- Keep away from harder stones (diamonds, sapphires)
- Avoid extreme temperature changes
- Store away from direct sunlight

**Professional Maintenance:**
- Annual inspection of prongs and settings
- Check for any loose diamonds
- Professional cleaning and polishing
- Ensure topaz is secure in setting
